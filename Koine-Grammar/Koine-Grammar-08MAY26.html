Noun Declension Exceptions

Standard first declension rule for stems ending in 'α'

If a first declension word has a stem ending in alpha, where the precedingletter is ᾽ε᾽/᾽ι᾽/᾽ρ᾽ it will form the genitive and dative singular in the standard way with ᾽α᾽. Otherwise the ᾽α᾽ will shift to ᾽η᾽ in the genitive and dative singular.

For example, for the feminine word δοξα, the declensions are as follows:

Sg. Pl.
nom δοξα δοξαι
gen δοξης* δοξων
dat δοξῃ* δοξαις
acc δοξαν δοξας

On the other hand ωρα follows the standard pattern because the stem ends in ρ

Sg. Pl.
nom ωρα ωραι
gen ωρας ωρων
dat ωρᾳ ωραις
acc ωραν ωρας
Standard first declension rule for stems ending in ᾽η᾽

All first declensions that have eta in the singular shift to alpha in the plural. In other words, the feminine plurals case endings are the same whether the stem ends in α or η

outos

oυτος, αυτη, τουτο, are demonstrative pronouns, meaning this, these

masc (2) fem (1) neut (2)
nom sg ου`τος αυ῾τη τουτο
gen sg τούτου ταύτης τουτου
dat sg τούτῳ ταύτῃ τουτῳ
acc sg τούτον ταύτην τουτο
- - -
nom pl ου`τοι αυ῾ται ταυτα
gen pl τούτων τουτων τούτων
dat pl τούτοις ταυταις τούτοις
acc pl τούτους ταυτας ταυτα

Peculiarity 1: In the sing. neut nominative and accusative, oυ`τος does not use a case ending. This is a normal sub-pattern for the neuter, also seen in the article 'το' and in 'αλλος'

Peculiarity 2: oυτος always begins with a rough breathing or tau. Think of the two as interchangeable. This is important in distinguishing the feminine αυται from the fem nom pl of αυτος which always has a smooth breathing.

Peculiarity 3: The first stem vowel used depends upon the final stem vowel

  • If the final vowel is α or η (the feminines except the gen. pl.), the form will have αυ in the stem. e.g: ταυταις, ταύτης
  • If the final vowel is ο, the stem will have ου e.g. τούτου

Notes (on tense formatives):

  • The Presents don't use a tense formative but use a connecting vowel
  • The Futures use both a tense formative and a connecting vowel
  • The Aorists use either a tense formative or a connecting vowel
  • The Perfects use a tense formative in the Active and no tense formative in middle/passive; but never use the connecting vowel

Notes (on augments):

  • All tenses that occur in the past use an augment or reduplications
  • The Imperfects are like the presents except for the augment

Notes (on personal endings):

  • The future and the present and the perfect always use the primary endings
  • The imperfect and the aorist always use the secondary endings
