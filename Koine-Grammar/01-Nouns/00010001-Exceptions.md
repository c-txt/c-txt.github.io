
# Noun Declension Exceptions

###### Standard first declension rule for stems ending in 'α'
If a first declension word has a stem ending in alpha, where the precedingletter is **᾽ε᾽/᾽ι᾽/᾽ρ᾽** it will form  **the genitive and dative singular** in the standard way with ᾽α᾽. Otherwise the ᾽α᾽ will shift to ᾽η᾽ in **the genitive and dative singular**.
  
For example, for the feminine word δοξα, the declensions are as follows:

|   |  Sg. |  Pl. |
|---|---|---|
|  nom |  δοξα |  δοξαι |
|  gen |  **δοξης*** |  δοξων |
|  dat |  **δοξῃ*** |  δοξαις |
|  acc |  δοξαν |  δοξας |

On the other hand ωρα follows the standard pattern because the stem ends in ρ

|   |  Sg. |  Pl. |
|---|---|---|
|  nom |  ωρα |  ωραι |
|  gen |  ωρας |  ωρων |
|  dat |  ωρᾳ |  ωραις |
|  acc |  ωραν |  ωρας |

###### Standard first declension rule for stems ending in ᾽η᾽

All first declensions that have eta in the singular shift to alpha in the plural. In other words, the feminine plurals case endings are the same whether the stem ends in α or η
